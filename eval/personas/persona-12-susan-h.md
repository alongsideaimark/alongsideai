### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work? (or if retired, what did you did?)**

I taught middle school English for 26 years and then served as principal of two different middle schools for another 11 years. I retired in 2019. My "work" now is the memoir project I've been chipping away at, the book club I host the third Thursday of every month, and helping my husband with our small acreage outside Burlington.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

No. Though I sometimes joke that the memoir is a business given how much it resists me.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

Mornings are mine. I write from about 8 to 11, when I'm able to. Afternoons are errands, garden work in season, walks with our dog Hattie, and reading. Evenings my husband Phil and I read or watch something. I have grandchildren in town twice a week and the rhythm of those visits punctuates the week pleasantly.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Anything to do with sharing files. My writing group meets every other week and we all email Word documents back and forth and I cannot keep track of which version is current. I know there are better ways and I have not learned them.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The cloud. Both of our laptops are constantly asking me to log in to iCloud or back up to OneDrive or sync with Dropbox and I do not understand which is which or what is happening. My son has tried to explain it to me three times. I think I just need someone to do it once and tell me what to do from there.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Organizing my writing. I have hundreds of files — drafts, fragments, scenes I wrote and abandoned, notes from old journals I've been transcribing. Some are in Word, some in Pages, some in Apple Notes, some are still in spiral notebooks I haven't typed up. I cannot find anything when I want it.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Yes — every time I lose a draft. I once worked on a chapter for two weeks and saved it in a place I could not find for three months. I wept.

### Section 4 — Tools You Use Today

**What devices do you use?** Laptop, phone, tablet

**iPhone or Android?** iPhone (12)

**Mac or PC?** Mac. MacBook Air. We also have an old iMac in the kitchen we use for recipes.

**What apps or software do you use for work?**

Apple Mail, Apple Calendar, Apple Notes. Microsoft Word for the memoir. Pages occasionally. Goodreads for my reading and the book club. Substack — I subscribe to several but don't write one. iCloud, which I do not understand.

**How do you keep track of your schedule, to-do list, or notes?**

A weekly paper planner I love. I write everything in it. Apple Calendar for things Phil and I share. Notes I take in spiral notebooks and sometimes type up.

**How do you communicate with clients or customers?**

I have no clients. I email the book club. I text my children and grandchildren. Old colleagues I email and occasionally see.

### Section 5 — What You've Tried

**Have you tried any AI tools before?** A little — ChatGPT once

**If yes, what was your experience?**

I asked it about a passage I was struggling with — whether the rhythm worked. It said pleasant things that felt like the things you'd say to a child showing you a finger painting. I closed it.

**Is there anything about AI that makes you nervous or skeptical?**

I worry about authenticity in writing. The memoir is mine — my voice, my memories, my mistakes. I would not want a tool that smooths it into something palatable. I have read enough to know that's a real risk.

### Section 6 — What "Better" Looks Like

**If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how you work, what would it be?**

A clean, organized writing system where every fragment, every chapter draft, every note is findable. And where I trust that I won't lose work.

**How much time per week do you think you lose to tasks that feel inefficient?**

A few hours. I'm retired. But the writing time I lose to disorganization is the time I'd most like to recover.

**Are you looking for help with your business, your personal life, or both?**

Personal life. Specifically the writing project.
