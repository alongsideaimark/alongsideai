### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I own two dry cleaners. Royal Oak and Berkley.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yeah. Took it over from my dad in 2003. He started the Royal Oak store in '78. We added Berkley in 2011, which I'm still paying for. Eleven employees between the two — counter staff, pressers, one guy who does our routes for the businesses we pick up at. My wife Denise does the books. We do dry cleaning, alterations, wash-and-fold, some commercial accounts (couple restaurants, a couple medical offices). About $850K a year. Could be more if I figured out how to grow it. Hasn't really grown in ten years.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I open Royal Oak at 6:30, usually I'm there till 11. Then I drive over to Berkley, deal with whatever's broken or whoever didn't show up. Afternoons I'm running between the two or out at customer accounts dropping off and picking up. Home around 7. Saturdays I work too. Sunday is supposed to be off but the phone still rings.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Tracking down lost tickets. Customer comes in says "I dropped something off three weeks ago." We have to look through the paper logs and hope the tag is still on a hanger somewhere. We use a POS called SPOT but half the time the staff doesn't enter things right and the search doesn't help.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The website. I have one. Some kid built it for me in 2014 for $400. I don't touch it. People ask if we have online ordering or pickup scheduling and we don't and I don't know how to add it.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Reminding customers to come pick up their stuff. We have racks of clothes from people who dropped off in March and never came back. Half of them are still here. Eats up space and we can't just throw it out.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Hiring. I cannot keep counter staff. Indeed, Facebook, putting a sign in the window — I do all of it and they last six weeks. I don't know if it's me or if it's the business.
