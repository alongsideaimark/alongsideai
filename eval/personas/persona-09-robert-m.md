### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I'm semi-retired. I had a 35-year career in commercial banking — credit, then commercial lending, then commercial banking leadership at a regional bank in the Northeast. I retired from full-time work in 2022 and have been doing consulting work for community and mutual banks roughly one to two days a week since then. The work is primarily credit policy review, problem loan workouts, and occasional executive coaching for newer commercial lenders.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

I have an LLC I set up for the consulting work. It is a sole proprietorship in practice — me and a small bookkeeper I share with my wife's separate business. I take on one or two engagements at a time, usually three to six months in length, billed monthly. Revenue last year was approximately $180,000, which is intentional. I am not trying to build something.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

When I am working, mornings are usually phone calls or video conferences with the bank I am engaged with. Afternoons are reading — credit memos, policy documents, financial statements, whatever the engagement requires. I take Wednesdays off entirely. Most weeks I work three or four days.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Reviewing long credit packages. A typical commercial credit memo is forty to one hundred pages, and I am often reviewing five or six at a time. I have a way of doing it that I have refined over thirty years, but it remains time-consuming.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The administrative side of running my LLC. Quarterly tax payments, the filings, dealing with my own invoicing. My wife handles most of it because she runs her own business and is competent at it. I am not.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Scheduling. Each engagement requires meetings with five or six bank executives whose calendars do not overlap. The back-and-forth of finding a time is more annoying than the meetings themselves.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Document review for credit memos. I have read tens of thousands of these in my career. The structure is reasonably consistent — financial summary, industry analysis, collateral analysis, repayment sources, risk rating rationale. I have wondered whether there is a way to extract the key data points more efficiently and use my reading time on the parts that require judgment.
