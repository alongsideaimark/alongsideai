### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I own Anastasi Jewelers. We have two locations — one in South Tampa where the original store is, and a newer location in Carrollwood we opened in 2018. Fine jewelry, engagement rings, watches, custom design work, repairs.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes. My father started it in 1976 in a little corner shop on Howard Avenue. I came in after college in '99 and took over the day-to-day around 2010 when his health started slipping. He's still alive, 82, still comes in on Saturdays and asks why we changed the lighting. Six employees between the two stores — three sales associates, a goldsmith who's been with my dad since 1988, a part-time bookkeeper, and my wife who runs the Carrollwood store. We do about $1.6M a year. About 40% engagement rings, 30% other fine jewelry, 20% watch sales, 10% repairs and custom work.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I'm at the South Tampa store most days, usually in by 9:30 since we open at 10. Mornings I'm checking inventory, dealing with vendors, working on whatever custom jobs are in progress. Afternoons are appointments — engagement ring consultations are mostly afternoons or evenings, that's a ninety-minute conversation typically. I close out the register at 6 and usually I'm out by 7. Saturdays I work in Carrollwood with my wife.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Inventory. We have probably 4,000 SKUs across the two stores and our system — we use Edge — is okay but not great. Reconciling what's actually in the cases with what the system says we have is a whole-day project that nobody wants to do, so it doesn't get done.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Our website. We have one. It's basically a phone book listing — hours, location, photos. Half my customers under 40 ask if they can buy online and I have to tell them no. I don't even know how to start fixing it. Building a real e-commerce site for jewelry feels like a different business.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Customer follow-up. After someone buys an engagement ring, they're going to want a wedding band in three to six months. We don't have a system to remind them or remind us. Same thing with anniversary purchases. We rely on memory and goodwill.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Photographing inventory. We have a guy who comes in once a quarter and shoots everything for our Instagram and the website. It's expensive and it's always behind real-time. Half the pieces he photographed last quarter are sold by the time the photos come back.

### Section 5 — What You've Tried

**Have you tried any AI tools before?** Yes — ChatGPT a few times

**If yes, what was your experience?**

I used it once to write a description of a piece for our Instagram — it was actually really good. My niece uses it for that now. I tried asking it about diamond grading once and it gave me an answer that was technically correct but oversimplified.

**Is there anything about AI that makes you nervous or skeptical?**

I worry about it generating photos of products we don't have. I'd never want a customer to see something on our website and come in to find it doesn't exist. And I worry about it pretending to be me on email or text — my customer relationships are personal and I don't want them feeling like they're talking to a machine.
