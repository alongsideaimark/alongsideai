### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I'm the superintendent of a small-to-mid-sized public school district in the Eugene area. About 4,200 students, six elementary schools, two middle schools, one high school. I've been superintendent for six years. Before that I was a high school principal for nine years and a teacher for twelve before that.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

No. I work for an elected school board.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I'm in the office by 7:30, usually out at one of the schools by 9 — I try to be in every building at least twice a month. Afternoons I'm in meetings — with my admin team, with principals, with the district office staff, with community members, occasionally with the school board chair. Evenings vary — board meetings twice a month run until 10pm. Other evenings I'm home for dinner, then I'm catching up on email until I fall asleep at my desk.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Email and parent communication. I get hundreds of emails a day. Many are routine, many are forwarded from somewhere they should have been handled, and a small but real number are crises that need my immediate attention. Sorting which is which is a daily exercise.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The personal side of life. My wife Sarah handles a lot of our household admin — bills, kids' college stuff, our taxes — and I am embarrassingly absent from it. She's been on me to step up and I keep meaning to.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Drafting routine communications. Every week I have to write a community update, a board update, responses to a few parents who have written in, sometimes a letter for a family or staff situation. Most of these have a similar shape and I keep starting from a blank page.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Yes — every time we have a crisis. School lockdown, weather closure, bus accident, anything that requires fast communication to staff, families, and the community. We have a system (it's called Blackboard Connect) that does mass notifications, but the actual writing of the message in the moment is on me, and I'm doing it under pressure with shaking hands. There has to be a better way to prepare for that.
