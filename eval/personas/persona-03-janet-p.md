### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I own a bookkeeping and small-business tax practice in Iowa City. We do monthly bookkeeping, payroll filings, and individual and business tax returns for about 110 clients across eastern Iowa. I'm not a CPA — I've been an enrolled agent since 1992.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes, since 1996. Started at my kitchen table after I left a regional accounting firm. Eight people now, including me — two senior bookkeepers, two junior, a payroll specialist, an admin, and a part-time tax-season helper. Most of our clients are small businesses with 5 to 30 employees: dental practices, a couple of restaurants, several farm operations, contractors, a few retail shops. The plan is to retire in roughly three years. I have a buyer in mind — one of my senior bookkeepers — but the firm needs to be in better shape before I hand it over.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I'm in the office by 7:30. Mornings I review work the team has prepared — payroll filings, monthly closes, tax returns during season. Afternoons I take client calls and meetings. I still have about 20 clients I personally manage end-to-end and probably should not, but I haven't gotten around to handing them off. Tax season I'm there until 7 or 8pm and most Saturdays. The rest of the year is more reasonable, six to four-thirty.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Client onboarding for new bookkeeping engagements. There's an engagement letter, a request for prior-year returns, bank statements, payroll reports, chart of accounts review, an intake meeting. The whole process is a back-and-forth email chain that takes three to six weeks per new client. I know there's software that does this but I haven't sat down to evaluate it.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The marketing side. I have a website that's about ten years old. I do not understand SEO, I do not have a real online presence, and most of our new clients come from referrals. I keep meaning to fix it and I keep not.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Year-end 1099 prep. Every January I have a panic week chasing W-9s from clients' vendors and getting 1099s out the door. Some of our clients use Bill.com which makes it easier; the ones who don't are a nightmare.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Document collection from clients. We use ShareFile, but clients still email statements, drop off paper, text photos of receipts. Tax season is half spent organizing what people sent us. I've watched my younger staff get good at this and I marvel at it because I cannot.
