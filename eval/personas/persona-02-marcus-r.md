### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I own Sunburst HVAC. We do residential heating and cooling — repairs, replacements, new installs, maintenance plans. Phoenix metro and East Valley.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes. Started in 2011 after ten years working for two other shops. Twenty-five people now: ten field techs, three apprentices, four office staff, two installers, two warehouse, dispatcher, sales rep, and my brother who runs the install crew. We did $4.2M last year. Phoenix summers are our Q4 — June through September is when the year is made or broken.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

In the truck or in the office. I'm out at job sites probably three days a week — quoting big installs, troubleshooting calls the techs can't crack, checking on training. Office days I'm running payroll prep with the bookkeeper, doing financial reviews, pricing maintenance contracts. Phone is constant. I'm up at 5, usually wrap by 7pm. Saturdays are emergency-only but I'm always reachable.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Quoting. Specifically, putting together quotes for full system replacements. The tech goes out, takes notes, photos, measurements. Then it gets dropped on me or one of the office girls to turn into a real quote with equipment specs and pricing. We're using ServiceTitan but the quote workflow still takes 30 to 45 minutes per quote and I do five to ten of those a week myself.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Reviewing my own marketing. I have a contractor running our Google Ads and a separate guy doing Facebook. They send reports. I look at them, can't really tell what's working, sign off on the next month's spend. I know I'm overpaying somewhere but I can't tell where.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Customer follow-up after a service call. Right now it's a manual checkbox in ServiceTitan that someone is supposed to do — call back two days later, see if everything's running. We do it maybe 40% of the time. I want it automatic.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Maintenance plan renewals. We have like 1,400 customers on annual maintenance plans. The renewal cards go out by mail — actual mail — and we chase the holdouts manually. Renewal rate is around 70% which is fine but we leak money every year on the 30% who would have stayed if someone had texted them.
