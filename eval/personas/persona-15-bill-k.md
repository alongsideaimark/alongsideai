### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

Right now? Not much. I sold my company in November of last year. I'm in a two-year non-compete that prevents me from going back into manufacturing. So I'm in this strange period where I have time and money and not much to do with either.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

I owned a precision metals manufacturing business in Ohio for 22 years. We made specialty fasteners and small parts for the medical device industry. Maxed out at 47 employees. I sold to a private equity rollup in November for an amount that has me set if I don't do anything stupid. My wife Linda and I moved to Naples right after closing. Two kids, both grown, both back in Ohio.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

Up at 6, on the bike for an hour, breakfast with Linda. Then I'm... figuring out what to do. I've been looking at residential real estate as a possible next move — I've bought three single-family rental properties in Naples since we moved, and I'm looking at a fourth. I read a lot more than I ever did. I'm taking a Spanish class twice a week. Honestly, most days have too much white space.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Researching real estate. I'm looking at properties on Zillow, on Redfin, in the Naples MLS through an agent, and through a couple of off-market wholesalers. Information is everywhere and nowhere. I want to look at numbers — cap rates, comparable sales, rental comps — and there's no clean way to do it.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The financial side of the rental properties. I have an LLC for each property, separate bank accounts, a property manager, a CPA who's reasonable. The infrastructure is fine. But pulling it all together into one view of how the portfolio is doing — I haven't built that and I'm losing financial visibility I had naturally with the manufacturing business.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Property management coordination. I have a property manager who handles tenant issues, but the back-and-forth between her, my CPA, my insurance person, and the various vendors is more than I expected.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Tracking my own time. I went from working 70-hour weeks to working maybe 15-hour weeks and I have no sense of where the rest of the time is going. I keep meaning to figure out what I actually do all day and I keep not.
