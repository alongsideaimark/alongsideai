### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I run a commercial salmon fishing boat in Bristol Bay, Alaska. Drift gillnet, sockeye salmon mostly, with a small kings and chums quota. The season runs roughly mid-June through late July with the heaviest fishing in two and a half intense weeks in early July.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes. I own my permit, my boat (a 32-footer named the Coral Marie), and my gear. Six-person crew including me. Last season I grossed about $680,000 — gross, before crew shares, fuel, ice, processor fees, all the rest. Net to me was around $140,000, which was an above-average year. We deliver to a regional processor. The business is essentially: prep all year for six weeks of fishing, fish hard, recover, repeat.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

In season, the day is whatever the tide and the fish demand. Sleep when you can. Out of season, I'm in Anchorage with my husband Dave. I do gear repair in our garage in the spring, financial reconciliation with my accountant in fall, permit and tax filings in winter. I teach a seasonal navigation class through the local community college. I read, I ski, I see family.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Off-season: financial reconciliation. Six weeks of fishing produce about 600 transactions across ice, fuel, parts, crew shares, wholesale settlements, gear, permit fees, vessel maintenance. Reconciling all of that against my QuickBooks file takes me an embarrassing amount of time every fall.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Marketing the boat for crew. I post ads in the Alaska fishing forums and on a couple of regional Facebook groups when I'm hiring, which is most years. Finding good crew is genuinely hard. I'm not a marketer.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Communicating with my crew in the off-season. I have a six-person crew that is partially returning, partially new each year. Pre-season we have a million logistics — gear assignments, travel, paperwork, insurance, advance payments. It's email and text and phone calls and inevitably someone is missing critical information when we head out.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Tracking maintenance and gear inventory. The boat is a complex piece of equipment. The gillnet gear (nets, lines, weights, hangings) deteriorates and needs replacing on a rotation I keep in my head. I lose money every year on gear failures that were predictable.

**iPhone or Android?** iPhone (12, beat to hell)

QuickBooks Online for accounting. Excel for everything QuickBooks can't do. Google Workspace. Square for the rare in-person sales we do. Garmin chartplotter software. ChatGPT — I have a free account I tried a few times.

Two paper notebooks — one for the boat, one for everything else. Apple Calendar for things Dave and I both need. Apple Notes for quick capture.

**How do you communicate with clients or customers?**

The processor (my main "customer") I deal with through the portal and by phone. My crew I email and text year-round. Other captains I see at the docks and at industry meetings in the winter.

### Section 5 — What You've Tried

**Have you tried any AI tools before?** A little — ChatGPT a few times

**If yes, what was your experience?**

I asked it to help me draft a difficult email to a former crew member who I wasn't going to invite back. It produced something usable. I asked it about salmon market projections once and got a pretty generic answer.

**Is there anything about AI that makes you nervous or skeptical?**

In a remote-rural business, half the AI hype is irrelevant to me. I'm not worried about it — I'm just skeptical of how much of it would actually apply to a fishing operation. I'd rather a small number of recommendations that make sense for my context than a generic small-business plan.

### Section 6 — What "Better" Looks Like

**If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how you work, what would it be?**

A reliable system for crew communication and pre-season logistics — so when the season starts, the six of us are aligned and nothing's been forgotten. The actual fishing is fine. The orchestration around the fishing is where I leak time and energy.

**How much time per week do you think you lose to tasks that feel inefficient?**

In the off-season, maybe 6 hours. In season, irrelevant — the day is what it is.

**Are you looking for help with your business, your personal life, or both?**

Business. The personal life I've got handled.
