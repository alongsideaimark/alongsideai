### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I'm a residential architect, semi-retired. I had a small practice in Santa Fe for thirty years — high-end custom homes, mostly, in the local vernacular. I dissolved the firm in 2022 and now I take one or two small projects a year, usually for past clients or referrals from past clients.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

I have a sole proprietorship for the projects I still do. No employees. I subcontract structural engineering and CAD production when needed. Revenue last year was about $90,000. The work is enough to keep me engaged and not so much that it takes over.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

When I have a project active, mornings are at the desk — drawing, sketching, calling consultants. Afternoons sometimes a site visit. When I don't have a project, mornings are my own — I draw a lot, both for myself and for a watercolor practice I've been keeping for years. My wife Lourdes is a high school art teacher, retired three years ago, and we have a slow rhythm I've come to like.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Producing construction documents on my own. When I had the firm I had a draftsperson. Now when I take a project, I'm doing the CAD myself in AutoCAD or sometimes I subcontract it out, but the back-and-forth with the subcontractor adds days. I've been considering finally moving to Revit but the learning curve at this stage of my career feels like a poor investment.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Marketing of any kind. I have a website that hasn't been updated since 2019. I've never been on Instagram, despite my younger colleagues telling me for years that's where the work is.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Project management for the few projects I take. Coordinating with the contractor, the client, the engineers — the email chain on a single residential project can run to hundreds of messages and it's exhausting.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Tracking design ideas. I keep sketchbooks — I have probably 80 of them on a shelf — and the ideas I want to come back to are spread across two decades. I've thought many times about photographing or scanning them and creating some kind of searchable archive but I've never done it.
