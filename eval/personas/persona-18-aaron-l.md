No. I'm an employed physician at the hospital. My wife Anjali is also a physician — she's an internist in a hospital-employed practice across town. Three kids, ages 11, 9, and 5. We have a part-time nanny who covers the gaps in school schedules.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

Up at 5:30, in the OR by 6:45 most days. First case starts at 7:30, last case usually wraps between 4 and 6. Some days I'm on call overnight. **What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Family logistics. School pickup coordination, kids' activities (one daughter does competitive swim, son is in a youth orchestra, youngest is just hitting the age where things start), pediatric appointments, summer camp planning. We have a shared Google Calendar and it is constantly out of date.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Yes — my own clinical research. I've been trying to write up a case series for two years. The dataset is in Excel. The references are in EndNote. The draft is in Word. The figures are in GraphPad. Pulling it all together is a multi-week project I never quite get to.
