### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I run a small skincare brand called Field & Form. We do clean-formulation face care — cleanser, serum, moisturizer, a couple of masks. DTC primarily, on Shopify. Some wholesale, mostly independent boutiques in the PNW and a few in NYC.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes. Founded in 2019, full-time on it since 2020. We did about $1.1M last year, mostly DTC, growing maybe 20% YoY which is fine but not exciting. Three full-time employees: a head of ops, a content lead, and a customer experience person. Plus four contractors I rely on heavily — fractional CFO, freelance designer, a fulfillment partner that's basically an extension of the team, and a part-time email marketer who's been with us forever.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

It depends on the week. New product launch weeks are 12 hours of meetings, copy review, vendor calls, photo shoots. Normal weeks I try to keep mornings for actual work — financial review, copy writing, brand strategy stuff — and afternoons for meetings. I'm in Slack constantly, in Notion several times a day, and the one I keep saying I'll quit but don't is Shopify admin where I lose 90 minutes at a time.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Customer service triage. We get a lot of "is this product right for my skin" questions and Yumi (CX) does a great job, but the volume is real and we've never really templated her responses well. Same questions over and over, slightly different. I've been thinking about this for a year.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Reconciling our financial picture across systems. QuickBooks, Shopify, Recharge for subscriptions, Klaviyo for email-attributed revenue, the wholesale invoicing in a Google Sheet. My CFO does it monthly but the gap between her report and what I see in Shopify's dashboard always confuses me and I avoid digging in.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Influencer outreach and follow-up. We've gotten organic mentions from like 30 creators over the last year and I have no system to track them, build relationships, or convert them into something more durable. It all lives in my DMs.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Cross-tool sprawl. We use Shopify, Klaviyo, Recharge, Gorgias for support tickets, ShipBob for fulfillment, Notion for ops, ClickUp for projects, Slack for everything else, plus Google Workspace, plus QuickBooks, plus a B2B portal for wholesale, plus my email marketer's tools. I don't think we need to consolidate but the data doesn't talk and I'd love to actually see one dashboard.
