### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes — sole proprietorship under my name. I have about a dozen active client relationships at any time, with maybe four of those being my "anchor" clients I work with month after month. **What does a typical workday look like for you?**

Up at 6:30, get my daughter Maya ready and to school by 8. I work from a desk in my second bedroom from 8:30 to 2:30. Dinner, bedtime routine, sometimes I work another hour after she's asleep if I'm on a deadline. It works only because I've structured the work around it.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Client onboarding and project intake. Every new project starts with a flurry of emails establishing scope, getting brand assets, setting up shared folders, scheduling the kickoff. I do it manually each time and it's an hour or two of unbillable work per project that adds up.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Marketing my own business. I have an Instagram I post on inconsistently, a website I haven't updated in over a year, and a LinkedIn presence that exists in name only. New clients come from referrals because the rest is broken.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Project management on the larger engagements. The actual design work I love. The client follow-up, the round-of-revisions tracking, the asset delivery checklists, the invoicing — that's where I leak hours.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Pricing my projects. I quote projects from gut feel and historical comparison and I'm probably underpricing 30% of the time. I've tried a couple of "freelance pricing calculator" tools and none of them have worked for the kind of work I do.
