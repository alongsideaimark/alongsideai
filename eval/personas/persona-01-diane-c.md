### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I own and run Movement Forward Physical Therapy. We have three clinics around metro Atlanta — Marietta, Sandy Springs, and Roswell. I'm a PT by training but at this point I'm probably 80% running the business and 20% seeing patients.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

Yes. We started in 2009 with one location and a partner; he retired in 2017 and I've been solo since. Twelve employees now — four PTs including me, two PT assistants, three front desk staff, an office manager (Tracy, who's been with me for eleven years and is the actual reason the place runs), a part-time billing specialist, and a marketing contractor. Last year we did about $1.8M in revenue. We're outpatient ortho, mostly — knees, shoulders, post-surgical rehab, the occasional weekend warrior. Average patient comes in for eight to twelve sessions.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I'm at the Marietta location Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and I split Tuesday and Thursday between Sandy Springs and Roswell. Usually four to six patients in the morning, lunch is when I do email and management stuff, and afternoons are either more patients or admin. I leave between 5:30 and 6:30 most days. Some weekend hours doing payroll or pulling reports.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Insurance authorizations. Tracy spends most of Wednesday afternoon every single week chasing pre-auths — calling, faxing, waiting on hold. We have a billing specialist who handles the actual claims, but the auth piece sits with Tracy and it's a black hole. Honestly I'm not even sure exactly what she's doing during those hours, just that they disappear.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Quarterly financial review with my CPA. I dread it. He sends a P&L and I scan it and there's always something — utilities up 18%, supplies up 12%, whatever — that I should have caught a month earlier. By the time we're meeting it's history.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Staff scheduling across the three locations. Right now it lives in a shared Google Sheet that everyone edits and we have constant problems — two PTs scheduled for the same Saturday shift, a front desk hole nobody noticed until 9am Monday. I keep meaning to look at scheduling software but every demo I've done felt like overkill for our size.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Patient rebooking. We don't have a system to flag patients who haven't booked their next appointment. We just notice — or don't — and call them. I'd guess we're losing 15 to 20% of expected sessions just to people falling off our radar after the first or second visit.
