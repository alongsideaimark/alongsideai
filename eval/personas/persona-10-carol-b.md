### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work?**

I'm retired from pediatrics — I practiced for 38 years, the last 22 at a pediatric group practice in St. Paul. I retired in 2021. Now I do volunteer medical work twice a year in Honduras with a small NGO I've worked with since 1998, I serve on two nonprofit boards, and my husband Dan and I run a small family foundation we set up after my mother passed in 2019.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

The family foundation is the closest thing. It's not a business but it has the trappings — about $3M in assets, distributing roughly $120,000 a year to causes Dan and I care about. Mostly children's health, immigrant services, and our church. I do most of the operational work — grant letters, record keeping, board meetings (the board is me, Dan, and our two adult children) — and we have a CPA who handles the accounting and tax filing.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

Varies enormously. On a "normal" week I'm home in Minneapolis, I might have a board meeting, foundation correspondence, time with grandchildren (we have four now, all in town), a doctor's appointment of my own, a long walk, and reading. Twice a year I'm in Honduras for two weeks with the medical team. Three or four times a year Dan and I travel for pleasure. I am as busy as I want to be, which is busier than I expected.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Coordinating with my children. Our daughter Anne lives in Minneapolis and has three of the grandchildren; our son Mark lives in Denver and has the fourth. Between the four kids' schedules, our travel, doctor appointments, and family events, I spend an enormous amount of energy on a logistics problem I am not sure other families have.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

The foundation's board reporting. I prepare quarterly summaries for our family board meetings — what we gave to, what we considered, financials. I do it in Word and Excel and it's labor I dread. Our CPA prepares the annual filing but the board materials are mine.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Travel logistics. We travel a lot — both medical work and pleasure — and the booking, itinerary management, and document organization is endless. I once missed a flight because I had not actually opened a confirmation email and I assumed the time I remembered was correct.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Filing and finding things. We have been generating paper and digital records as a family for fifty years, and the foundation has its own paperwork going back to 2019. I cannot find anything when I need it. Dan is no help.

**Have you tried any AI tools before?** A little — ChatGPT once or twice, and Siri (which I don't think really counts)

**If yes, what was your experience?**

I asked ChatGPT to help me write a difficult letter to a grant applicant we were declining. It wrote something kinder than what I had drafted, and I used most of it. I was impressed. I have not gone back since.

I also worry about scammers using AI to imitate voices. We had a friend who got a fake-grandchild call last year.
