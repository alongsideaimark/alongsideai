**Name:** Margaret S.

### Section 2 — Your Work

**What do you do for work? (or if retired, what did you do?)**

I retired from the federal bench in 2020 after eighteen years as a district court judge. Prior to that I was an Assistant U.S. Attorney for fourteen years and in private practice for nine years before that. Since retirement I have done some mediation work, mentor a small number of younger lawyers, sit on a panel for the federal judicial center, and travel.

**Do you run your own business? Tell us about it.**

No. I do not have an LLC or any commercial entity. The mediation work I take is through a roster maintained by the local court, and the payments are processed accordingly.

**What does a typical workday look like for you?**

I do not have workdays in any traditional sense. I have weeks where I am quite busy — a mediation may take three or four days of my time including preparation, and I have several mentees who I meet with monthly. I have weeks where my husband and I are traveling and I am not engaged with anything formal. We have three adult children and seven grandchildren scattered across the country, and a substantial portion of my time is given to that.

### Section 3 — What's Frustrating

**What takes way longer than it should in your day?**

Booking and managing travel. My husband and I travel quite a bit and the volume of confirmations, itineraries, hotel cards, and rental car reservations is more than I can comfortably manage. I've missed appointments because I could not find the relevant email at the relevant moment.

**Is there anything you avoid doing because it's confusing or tedious?**

Anything that involves "syncing" or "the cloud." I do not understand it. My husband Walter does not understand it either. Our children try to help and I find their explanations more confusing than the problem.

**What do you wish someone would just handle for you?**

Photographs. We have approximately fifty thousand digital photographs across multiple devices, and I cannot find any of them. Walter and I have been talking for years about a project to organize the family photographs, including scanning the boxes of paper photographs in the basement, and we have made no progress.

**Have you ever thought "there has to be a better way to do this"? What was it about?**

Yes — the cumulative correspondence around our children's lives. Two of our children have growing families with various medical, educational, and logistical things they share with us, and it is all in disorganized email threads. I would have benefitted enormously from a real system thirty years ago.

### Section 4 — Tools You Use Today

**What devices do you use?** Phone, laptop

**iPhone or Android?** iPhone (12)

**Mac or PC?** Mac. We have a MacBook Air. It is the only computer in the house.

**What apps or software do you use for work?**

For the mediation work — Microsoft Word, Outlook, and Zoom. I read briefs in Adobe Acrobat. I keep my professional contacts in Apple Contacts. I do not use anything more elaborate.

**How do you keep track of your schedule, to-do list, or notes?**

A paper Filofax, which I have used since 1987. Apple Calendar for things Walter and I need to share. To-do lists on yellow pads.

**How do you communicate with clients or customers?**

Email, phone, and letter. Yes, letters — I write physical letters to several long-time correspondents and to my grandchildren.

### Section 5 — What You've Tried

**Have you tried any AI tools before?** Almost not at all

**If yes, what was your experience?**

My son sat me down and showed me ChatGPT during a visit at Christmas. He asked it to draft a letter, which it did. I found the result a bit smooth and lacking voice. He used a phrase like "you have to learn how to prompt it" and I observed that this struck me as the inverse of useful technology.

**Is there anything about AI that makes you nervous or skeptical?**

Several things. The hallucinated case citations my former colleagues on the bench have written about — federal judges have begun sanctioning attorneys for filing AI-generated briefs with fabricated authority. The privacy questions around what happens to information one types into these systems. The general displacement of judgment in fields where judgment is the entire point. I am open to being wrong about all of this and I am not categorical. But I am cautious.

**How much time per week do you think you lose to tasks that feel inefficient?**

Five hours, perhaps. I am retired, so the question is somewhat philosophical.
